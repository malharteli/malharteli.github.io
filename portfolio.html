Meredith Blane aka. Madame Frost

One of the ideas the team had for a character was a damaged Superhero who failed their team in the past. While our plot was set aside in the interest of completing the game, we did end up keeping Madame Frost in the final build

Lancel d'Ore aka. The Kid

When considering the many ways the player could affect the world the characters were in, we considered a long-range beam attack- the result was Laser Cowboy over here. Lancel was just a huge westerns nerd. As such, his costume was based off a cowboy costume he wore as a kid. It was a bit hard fitting the normally biege and red-toned colors of traditional cowboy garb to the cool palette the team decided to use for the player characters, but we managed to make it work in the end.

Magma Gorilla

At first look, it would be easy to think of Magma Gorilla as just a hero made of stone. Actually, its a guy in a suit. The reason he's called "Gorilla" is because in use, the suits arms are so heavy, he has to support the rest of his body upon the fists of the mechanical suit.

Vectorman

The first character implemented into Justice Force Tactics, Vectorman had the power to move twice in one turn. He's considered a B-lister because he's too slow to break the sound barrier
