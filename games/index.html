    Zom-D0S

    The world has fallen apart. Ethereal beasts are all that remain of humanity. But you have two tools to keep you alive in this hellscape- an iron drone, capable of fighting these hellions... and a comp straight from the 80s. Good Luck.

    Role: Art Director

    For this project, I served as the primary art director of a five-person team. Our group had been formed in a last minute scenario, and the result was an initially disorganized mess of an art team. As Art Director, it was my job to take the lead and establish a pipeline for all our asset creation. I was in charge of deciding everything: from the programs we used, to the resolutions we exported at, to the exact colors we were allowed to use. The end result is a game very reminiscent of old 80s vector-based games

    Red

    Created in GameMaker Studio, Red is a side-scrolling 2D action game that was developed as an experiment on game-based narrative

    Role: Background Artist

    As background artist for Red, I worked closely with the lead sprite artist to develop a style that was cohesive with the style of the character sprites. All static objects in the game were designed to match the youthful, spritely nature of the project, and had the project continued further, would have grown less saturated over time, with the outlines around objects growing blurry to match the morality of the player.

    Parasite Perry

    A Java game was produced with the intent of creating a new and interesting interface for players. In order to play the game, please unzip it to a folder and run the .bat file.

    Role: Primary Artist

    As the only artist on this team, I had to work in two workflows for the sake of time. The first was the art for the interface. The interface is based on images taken of the human brain, with each button being represented as a neuron of the parasite's victim's brain. The other was the pixel-based animations that represent the hapless parasite in its body suit.

    Justice Force Tactics

    In a city ravaged by crime and constant peril, one brave intrepid player must step up and organize the perfect superhero team to answer all the larger than life threats that hover over their beloved city. This game takes tactics game play mechanics and applies them to a superhero setting. Each hero has their own special move that allows them to manipulate the battlefield as the player sees fit.

    Role: Producer/Art Director

    For this capstone project, I quickly found myself taking the primary directive role of the game. Every presentation had to be carefully constructed, every member of the group had to be constantly checked upon. The problem is, the game was scoped far beyond our capabilities within the few months we had. Despite this, I still continue to code and alter the game, trying to reach that original concept we started with.
